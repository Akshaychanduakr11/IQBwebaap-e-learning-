Linear Algebra, Calculus, and Series Convergence

UNIT 01

Linear Algebra I: Systems & Eigenvalues

This unit builds the foundation for solving complex engineering systems through matrix manipulation.

  • Linear Dependence: A set of vectors is dependent if $c_1v_1 + c_2v_2 + ... = 0$ for non-zero constants.
  • Eigen Theory: Solving $(A - \lambda I)x = 0$ to find the scaling factors ($\lambda$) and invariant directions ($x$) of a matrix.
  • Iteration Method: Used for finding the Largest Eigenvalue. By repeatedly multiplying a trial vector by the matrix, the vector converges to the dominant eigenvector.
  • Decomposition:
    • Gaussian Elimination: Systematic row reduction to Upper Triangular form.
    • LU Decomposition: Factorizing $A = LU$. Useful for solving $Ax=B$ multiple times with different $B$ values.

UNIT 02

Linear Algebra II: Vector Spaces & SVD

Moving from basic matrices to abstract vector spaces and advanced factorization.

  • Vector Spaces: Exploring Basis (the skeleton of the space) and Dimension (the size of the basis).
  • Rank-Nullity Theorem: A fundamental balance: $\text{dim}(\text{Domain}) = \text{Rank} + \text{Nullity}$.
  • Inner Product Spaces: Introducing the concept of Norm (length) and Orthogonality.
  • Gram-Schmidt Process: Converting any basis into a set of perpendicular (orthonormal) vectors.
  • SVD (Singular Value Decomposition): Factoring any matrix into $U \Sigma V^T$. This is the "Gold Standard" for data noise reduction.

UNIT 03

Differential Calculus: Successive & Mean Value

Advanced derivation techniques and function approximations.

  • Leibnitz’s Rule: The general formula for the $n^{th}$ derivative of a product: $(uv)_n = \sum \binom{n}{r} u_{n-r} v_r$.
  • Series Expansion:
    • Taylor's: Predicting function values near a point $x=a$.
    • Maclaurin's: A specific case of Taylor's where $a=0$.
  • L’Hospital’s Rule: A powerful tool for solving limits that result in indeterminate forms like $0/0$ or $\infty/\infty$.
  • Maxima & Minima: Using the first and second derivative tests to find optimized points in engineering designs.

UNIT 04

Integral Calculus: Special Functions & Applications

Evaluating complex areas and volumes through specialized integration.

  • Beta & Gamma Functions: "The Euler Integrals" that solve factorials for non-integers and simplify trigonometric integrals.
  • Curve Tracing: Techniques to sketch Cartesian curves by analyzing asymptotes, symmetry, and points of inflection.
  • Definite Integral Applications:
    • Rectification: Finding the exact length of a curved wire.
    • Volume of Revolution: Calculating the volume of an object created by spinning a curve around the x or y-axis.

UNIT 05

Sequence & Series: Convergence Testing

Ensuring that mathematical approximations actually settle on a finite value.

  • Convergence Tests: Including the Ratio Test, Root Test, and Comparison Test to check if an infinite sum is finite.
  • Cauchy Sequence: A sequence where the difference between terms approaches zero as they progress to infinity.
  • Alternating Series: Using Leibniz’s Test to determine convergence when terms flip between positive and negative.
  • Absolute Convergence: A stronger form of convergence where the sum of absolute values also converges.
