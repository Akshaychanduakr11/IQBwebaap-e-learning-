Hardware Foundations for Computer Science

UNIT I: Analog Electronics & Components

Electronics is vital for CS as it defines how hardware processes electrical signals into data. This unit covers the physics of components.

  • Passive Components: Resistors (limit current), Capacitors (store charge), and Inductors (store magnetic energy).
  • Active Components: Diodes (rectification) and Transistors (switching/amplification).
  • Op-Amps: Operational Amplifiers are used for signal scaling and mathematical operations.
// Ohm's Law Application
Voltage (V) = Current (I) * Resistance (R);

UNIT II: Digital Logic & Circuits

How physical signals become Binary (0 and 1). This is the logic-gate architecture of modern CPUs.

  • Combinational: Logic depends only on current input (Adders, Multiplexers, Decoders).
  • Sequential: Logic with memory (Flip-Flops, Registers, Shift Registers).
  • Adders: Half Adder (2 bits) and Full Adder (3 bits) are the heart of Arithmetic Logic Units (ALU).

UNIT III: Microcontrollers & Sensors

Introduction to programmable hardware like the Arduino Platform and real-world interfacing.

  • Microcontrollers: Integrated circuits that contain a processor, memory, and I/O on one chip.
  • ADC / DAC: Converting real-world analog signals (like light) into digital bits and vice-versa.
  • Sensors: Temperature, LDR (Light), Ultrasonic, and Proximity sensors.

UNIT IV: Embedded Systems & IoT

Dedicated systems where hardware and software are tightly integrated for specific tasks.

UNIT V: Communication Systems

The evolution of data transfer from simple Serial cables to high-speed 5G networks.

  • Wireless Network Topologies: Star, Mesh, and Bus configurations.
  • Cellular Concepts: Frequency reuse and cell splitting in 4G and 5G Technology.
  • Protocols: Bluetooth, Wi-Fi (WLAN), and CDMA Technology.

Latches are level-triggered (respond to voltage level), while Flip-Flops are edge-triggered (respond only to the change/clock pulse).

Voltage (V) is electrical potential, Current (I) is flow of charge, and Resistance (R) is the opposition to flow. Relationship: V = I × R (Ohm's Law).

A Half Adder adds two single-bit binary numbers (A, B) and has two outputs (Sum, Carry). A Full Adder adds three bits (A, B, and Carry-in) and is used for multi-bit addition.

A Multiplexer is a combinational circuit that selects one of many input signals and forwards it to a single output line based on select lines. It is also known as a "Data Selector."

ADC converts continuous physical signals (like temperature from a sensor) into a discrete digital number so a microcontroller like Arduino can process the data.

1. Perception Layer: Sensors and actuators.
2. Network Layer: Data transmission (Wi-Fi, 5G).
3. Middleware Layer: Cloud and data processing.
4. Application Layer: User interface/App.

Serial: Data bits are sent one by one over a single wire (e.g., USB). Slower but works over long distances.
Parallel: Multiple bits are sent simultaneously over multiple wires. Faster but limited to very short distances.
